James Gakara: Family of Nakuru Doctor's Wife Deny Claims She Wanted to Travel Abroad, Leave Kids Alo

  • According to Roseline Odhiambo, an elder sister to Nakuru doctor's wife, the mother of two was not planning to travel abroad
  • She was responding to reports that James Gakara killed his children over an argument with his wife over her desire to travel abroad
  • Roseline said the doctor and his wife were on good terms and were in constant communication on the day he allegedly murdered his kids

The family of the Nakuru doctor's wife have denied reports that the woman was planning to travel abroad and leave her family by themselves.

Winnie Odhiambo was away in Nairobi when her husband James Gakara, a Nakuru-based gynaecologist, allegedly killed their two young children and attempted to take his own life.

Speaking to the press on Wednesday, September 22, Winnie's elder sister Roseline Odhiambo said it was false that the mother of two wanted to travel abroad adding the couple did not have any wrangles.

According to Roseline, Winnie had just registered at the Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology and on the Saturday, September 18, that the incident happened, she was in Nairobi for her classes.

"The stories going around that my sister was planning to go abroad are all lies. Her husband was really supporting her and she had even enrolled on classes at JKUAT," she said.

Constant communication

Winnie's sister added that the couple was on good terms on Saturday, and were in constant communication.

"The day of the deaths of the children, they had been in constant communication and Doc had even asked his wife to suggest where to take the children for their outing,” she added.

In their last conversation, Gakara asked Winnie not to travel back home to Nakuru as he was planning to travel to Nairobi with the children. He told her he would pick her up at Juja.

However, that was never to happen as on the night o Saturday, the gynaecologist is suspected to have drugged his two children Dylan, 5, and Karuana, 3.

He then allegedly tried to take his own life but was rushed to the Nakuru Level 5 Hospital where he died on Wednesday, September 22, after four days in the Intensive Care Unit.

Unfortunately, the mother of two was overwhelmed with emotions after learning the news of her husband's death and she is currently admitted to the hospital's High Dependency Unit.

“I had really waited for you to regain consciousness so that I can know the truth and find closure,” she cried.
