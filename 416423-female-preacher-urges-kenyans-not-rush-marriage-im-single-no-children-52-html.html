Female Preacher Urges Kenyans Not to Rush into Marriage: &quot;I'm Single, No Children at 52&quot;

  • Reverend Jacqueline Othoro said many young people are often left disappointed after failing to achieve milestones which society have put out as checkers for success
  • Othoro said for her case, she is 52 years old, single and without children but nevertheless, she is alive and happy
  • She noted the young people should be flexible with their goals and know that anyone can achieve anything at any age

A female pastor has advised young Kenyans not to get disappointed in life when they fail to reach certain milestones perceived as parameters for success by society.

Reverend Jacqueline Othoro of Purpose Centre Church said there was a tendency by Millenials to feel they have to achieve milestones such as marriage or financial prosperity by the time they are 30 and this expectation has often left many hurting.

Speaking on NTV's show Your World, the woman of God said she is 52 years old and was still single and childless but nonetheless, the most important thing was that she is alive and happy.

Othoro said she had a target to get married by the time she was aged 28 but she did not lose hope when her dream failed to come true.

"I am 52 years old, still single and with no children. I am alive and that's critical. I had a goal to get married by 28, I am 52 now and still single, I didn't die or lose anything. Learn to be flexible with goals that you can't control, I can't control someone coming into my life," she said.

Societal pressures

Othoro noted the youth should not be pressured to achieve certain things by the time they hit a certain age since times have changed.

"We are putting a lot of pressure on younger people. There are people who will hire a car for the weekend just to be seen driving so that people can see that they have a car," she said.

The preacher said while having goals is critical, it is also important to be flexible and remember that anyone can achieve anything at any age.

Advice to love birds

In a separate report, relationship expert Benjamin Zulu advised fans against showing off their love life on social media, especially for celebrities.

In a lengthy message on Facebook, the love doctor said this only invites evil into their lives and would make it difficult to join the dating scene if they were to break up in future.

Zulu said there is power in dating privately and getting married before showing off one's relationship online as the public has nothing to better their union.
