Chris Wamalwa Says Ford Kenya Pulling Out of NASA: &quot;ODM Stole Our Political Parties' Funds&quot;

  • Vocal Bungoma senator Moses Wetang'ula leads the Ford Kenya party, while the secretary-general is Kiminini MP Chris Wamalwa

  • During the 2017 General Election, ODM got 73 elected MPs, Jubilee (17), Wiper (23), ANC (14) and Ford Kenya (13)

Ford Kenya acting secretary-general Chris Wamalwa has said that the party will pull out of the National Super Alliance (NASA).

Speaking to Citizen TV on the morning of Tuesday, July 6, Wamalwa accused the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) top leadership of sidelining its other partners in the coalition.

Other NASA coalition partners

Other parties in the coalition include the Musalia Mudavadi-led Amani National Congress (ANC) and Kalonzo Musyoka's Wiper Kenya.

Bungoma senator Moses Wetang'ula leads the Ford Kenya party.

Wamalwa, who was part and parcel of the technical team heading the coalition NASA, said that one of the critical things they agreed was about political parties funds.

''The arrangement to beused to get these monies is that the registrar of political parties channels the funds to our big brother, ODM and ODM is supposed to have the onwards submission or channel to the other partner parties,'' Wamalwa explained.

ODM accused of political betrayal

He said following the 2017 General Election, ODM had betrayed the three political partners.

''Our big brothers ODM have betrayed us. They got the funds, but when we tried to engage them, they refused to share the funds with us. One of the agreements in NASA is when the three parties pull out, then NASA will be dead,'' Wamalwa said.

Wamalwa, who doubles as the Kiminini lawmaker, said that the process of the three partners withdrawing from NASA was firmly on.

''Some of us who were in the House leadership were kicked out, and the ODM party leader himself has been on record saying that NASA is dead, but when the finance is convenient, and we are heading to the 2022 General Election, they start running up and down that we want to revive NASA for their selfish interests,'' Wamalwa said.

Section 25 of the Political Parties Act states that funds shall be distributed as follows; 95% of the fund proportionately by reference to the total number of votes secured by each political party in the preceding General Election and 5% of the administrative expenses fund.

Number of elected MPs

In 2017, ODM got (73) elected MPs, Jubilee (17), Wiper (23), ANC (14) and FORD Kenya (13).

''Even when we were under CORD, we were getting the money, and the money was being channelled through ODM so that ODM is supposed to share. So the presidential votes that the candidate got it was because of the partners, the value they added so subsequently when you get these political parties fund which is channelled through that party it is supposed to share with the partners,'' Wamalwa added.

But ODM secretary-general Edwin Sifuna has laughed off the remarks and dared the three principals to proceed to court.

Sifuna said the three parties were free to sever ties with ODM, saying that they have nothing to lose as an outfit.

“We have never known what amount of money they are demanding from us; the legal and factual basis of that demand. So at least when the matter is in court, we will know for the first time what their claim against us is. So we welcome that cause of action,” he said.
