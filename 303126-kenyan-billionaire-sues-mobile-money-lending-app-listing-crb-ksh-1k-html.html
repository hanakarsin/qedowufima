Kenyan billionaire sues mobile money lending app for listing him on CRB over KSh 1k

- The billionaire had been listed on CRB by a mobile money lending company

- He learnt about the listing when he applied for a loan from a local company

- He never took the loan and the company confirmed it was an error

- His credit score was affected and now he is seeking for legal redress on the matter

A Kenyan business tycoon has taken to the courts to battle it out with a mobile money lending company that blacklisted his name with the Credit Reference Bureau (CRB) over KSh 1,000 loan that he never took.

All was well for billionaire Peter Nduati until when his airline ambulance company, Centric Air, applied for a loan from a local bank.

Nduati,who narrated his story on Twitter, said trouble started when he was at the final stage of receiving the loan. He was informed by the bank that he could not receive the money as he had already been blacklisted by CRB.

Surprised by the revelations, the businessman, who has never taken any loan from any mobile application, started to follow up and find out about the said loan.

"I am very particular in maintaining a good credit score coz I use leverage for investing. The bank could not disclose who had listed me and referred me to CRB," he said.

He went to CRB and was informed the company had blacklisted him in December 2018. To make matters worse, when he asked about the number that was used to list him, he discovered it was not even his.

Nduati who by 2015 had built himself KSh 3 billion fortune said he tried to inquire about whose number was linked to him leading to the blacklist but the money lender never disclosed the name.

“I then went back to CRB with that report , I was told that only the money lender could clear me and I had to write a dispute letter,” he said.

After a long back and forth engagement with the company, he was later called by CRB and informed the money lender had cleared his name and that it was an error.

“No further explanation but that transaction has brought my credit score to BB- or something like that which simply means I have a 40 per cent chance of defaulting,” he added.

He has now sought legal redress on the matter as the blacklisting affected his ability to take more loans.

Nduati is the founder and CEO of Resolution Health Insurance Company and runs the PineCreek Records and Trueblaq Entertainment.
