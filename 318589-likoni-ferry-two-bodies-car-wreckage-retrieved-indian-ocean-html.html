Likoni ferry: Car, bodies finally retrieved from Indian Ocean after 13 days

- Relatives, friends and the public camped at the Likoni ferry to see bodies of Mariam Kighenda and daughter Amanda arrive on the shore later on Friday, October 11

- The exercise was delayed severally over heavy traffic and strong ocean currents

- The car was located on Wednesday, October 9, after 11 days of search in the Indian Ocean

- A veteran Swedish diver had offered to retrieve the two bodies within two hours but failed

- A team of divers from South Africa and Kenya Navy later located the car with the two bodies

Kenyans breathed a sigh of relief after a car which plunged into the Indian Ocean with two occupants on Sunday, September 29, was retrieved.

The vehicle was retrieved on Friday, October 11, approximately 13 days after the drowning incident which has received wide press coverage across the country.

Hundreds of Kenyans trooped to the scene to watch the retrieval process as journalists were forced to move away from the scene.

The vehicle bearing registration number KCB 286C carrying bodies of Miriam and her daughter Amanda was pulled out of the ocean at around 4:15pm after a delicate process which took hours to execute.

Kenya Navy commander Levi Mghala and Mombasa Governor Hassan Joho were among government officials present at the scene.

Government spokesperson Cyrus Oguna announced that the military would conduct a special service before the bodies are handed over to the family.

A crane which arrived at the scene earlier in the day lifted the vehicle to the shore as members of the Kenya Red Cross were at hand with three body bags.

The late Mariam's husband, John Wambua and members of his family was present at the scene during the retrieval ahead of burial plans.

On Wednesday, October 9, Oguna announced Kenya Navy divers had spotted a car thought to be the one which sunk with two occupants 57 metres deep inside the Indian Ocean.
