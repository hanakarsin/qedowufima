Musalia Mudavadi takes campaign for 2022 presidential bid to the US

- The ANC leader will be in the US for about two weeks during which he will meet Kenyans living in the diaspora

- Mudavadi is expected to give lectures at Massachusetts University and the Chamber of Commerce of Boston

- The former vice president will also hold meetings in Washington DC on September 30 and October 1 and meet the Kenyan business community in Atlanta

- He had in June 2019 declared his interest in the 2022 race to State House making it clear he will not play second fiddle again

Amani National Congress (ANC) party leader Musalia Mudavadi has travelled to the United States of America (USA) for a two-week tour to popularise his 2022 presidential ambition in the diaspora.

Mudavadi who was accompanied by several members of his party, including Secretary General Barrack Muluka, MPs Alfred Agoi (Sabatia), Bunyasi Sakwa (Nambale) and former Lamu governor Issa Timamy, landed in Boston on the night of Tuesday, September 24.

The ANC leader is expected to meet Kenyans living in New York and New Jersey and give lectures at Massachusetts University and the Chamber of Commerce of Boston during the tour according to sources privy to the details of the trip.

“He will hold meetings in Washington DC on September 30 and October 1 and meet the Kenyan business community in Atlanta on October 2 and 3,” Vihiga MP Alfred Agoi told The Standard.

Mudavadi in June 2019 officially declared his interest in the 2022 race to State House, joining the already long list of presidential hopefuls.

The ANC leader who is not new to the high-stakes competition, having unsuccessfully vied for the presidency in 2013 after a temporary fallout with ODM leader Raila Odinga, is certain he will be the man to beat.

The former deputy prime minister made it known he will not play second fiddle to anyone again come 2022.

"I have always been a selfless leader, ready to support others. This led to my founding of NASA as a means to give the country a new leadership that would move the country forward. Come 2022 I have decided to throw my hat in the ring so as to get the opportunity to serve Kenyans," he tweeted.

The presidential race has so far attracted a number of candidates, with each believing they have what it takes to succeed President Uhuru Kenyatta.

Deputy President William Ruto, the presumed Jubilee party candidate, is widely perceived as the front-runner in the next poll, with many, despite rift in the ruling party, still expecting Uhuru to endorse him as a way of returning the favour for the DP's unwavering support in 2013 and 2017.
