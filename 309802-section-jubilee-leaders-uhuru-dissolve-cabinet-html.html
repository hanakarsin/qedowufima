Section of Jubilee leaders want Uhuru to dissolve Cabinet

- The politicians allied to Kieleweke faction of the ruling party argued the current Cabinet would not be able to deliver on Big Four agenda because of division

- They pointed accusing fingers at DP William Ruto as being responsible for the growing discord in the Jubilee party

- The Kieleweke leaders also rubbished allegations that a section of Cabinet secretaries from Mount Kenya met to plan how to eliminate Ruto

A section of political leaders allied to the Team Kieleweke faction of the Jubilee party have called on President Uhuru Kenyatta to dissolve his Cabinet citing growing disharmony and mistrust within the government as the basis for dissolution.

The politicians among them nominated MP Maina Kamanda and Kiambu deputy governor James Nyoro claimed the Cabinet as currently constituted would not deliver on the Big Four agenda because of the sharp division.

Speaking at the ACK Riabai Church in Kiambu county on Sunday, July 7, the Kieleweke leaders suggested Uhuru should reconstitute the entire Cabinet so that he can have people who would effectively help him deliver on his development agenda.

“The current Cabinet is already divided. If I was him (the president), I would dissolve the entire Cabinet and constitute a new one to have 100% loyalty from the Cabinet members,” said Kamanda.

The nominated MP took a swipe at Deputy President William Ruto who he blamed for the sharp divisions in the Jubilee party.

He also rubbished claims a section of CSs from Mount Kenya region met at Hotel La Mada to plan on how to eliminate the DP so they could stop him from becoming president in 2022.

Deputy governor Nyoro echoed Kamanda's sentiments and accused Ruto of being disrespectful to the president by engaging in 2022 politics at the expense of service delivery and development.

“We cannot focus on campaigns until 2022. We are following what the president is saying; and that is shelving 2022 politics,” said Nyoro.

The current political stalemate and discord in the ruling party was escalated by the Ruto assassination claims in which CSs James Macharia (Transport), Joseph Mucheru (ICT), Sicily Kariuki (Health) and Peter Munya (Trade) were implicated.

Whereas members of the Kieleweke faction held a letter at the centre of the alleged murder plot was fabricated by the DP's aides to attract sympathy votes, the Ruto-led Tanga Tanga squad insisted there could be a real plan to get rid of their preferred 2022 presidential candidate.

The political storm triggered by Ruto's alleged murder plot pitted the DP and his allies against the accused CSs who are appointees of the president.
