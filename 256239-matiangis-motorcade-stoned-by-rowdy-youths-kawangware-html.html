Matiang'i's motorcade stoned by rowdy youths in Kawangware

- Angry youths attacked journalists after Acting Interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang'i visited a school in Kawangware

- The youths also blocked gates of Gatina Primary School after Matiang'i failed to address them during the visit

- Police dispersed the youths with tear gas after they damaged several vehicles near the school

Police on Monday, October 30, battled angry youths in Nairobi's Kawangware area after acting Interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang'i visited a school in the area.

The youths were angered when the Education CS visited Gatina Primary School to assess the preparations being made for KCPE exams.

They demanded that Matiang'i addresses them and when he failed to do so, they blocked the school's gate and pelted vehicles with stones, damaging several of them.

Journalists covering KCPE preparations were also not spared as the rowdy youths pelted them with stones, forcing majority to scamper for safety.

A vehicle belonging to Standard Group was damaged by the youths. The youths wanted to use pupils as shield to battle the police.

The fresh chaos come days after violence erupted in the area when youths from NASA and Jubilee clashed.

As reported by TUKO.co.ke, three people were killed in the skirmishes.

Police had a hard time containing the riots that also so property in millions of shillings burnt and looted.

There have been pockets of violence in the country since the repeat election was held on Thursday, October 26.

The NASA opposition boycotted the election and urged its supporters to keep off polling stations.
