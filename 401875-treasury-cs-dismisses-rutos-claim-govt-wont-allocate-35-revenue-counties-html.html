Treasury CS dismisses Ruto's claim gov't won't be able to allocate 35% revenue to counties

Treasury Cabinet Secretary (CS) Ukur Yatani has dismissed Deputy President William Ruto's claim that the national government wold not be able to allocate 35% of shared revenue to counties as proposed under the 2020 Constitutional amendment Bill.

The CS argued the country was in a position to disburse the funds if the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) sails through in the referendum.

Yatani defended the 35% proposal noting it was reached upon based on Kenya's projected economic growth.

"The amount of 30% or 35% is doable. Those saying that we have not given counties 15%, we have done even more," the senior Treasury official said at a meeting at his office.

Devolution CS Eugene Wamalwa also stated the government led by President Uhuru Kenyatta who has been at loggerheads with his deputy was committed to enhancing development in counties through the increment.

"You cannot question the commitment of this government to devolution. On average, we have never paid less than 15%," he insisted.

Ruto, now considered an outsider in the Jubilee administration after the famous March 2018 handshake by his boss Uhuru and former prime minister Raila Odinga, had laughed off a BBI proposal that seeks to raise budgetary allocation to counties from 15% to 35%.

The DP described the recommendation as ridiculous, citing the national government's inability to wire the current 15% budget allocation to the devolved units on time.

"They are talking about raising the amount of money sent to counties to 35% of the national government's budget. How will this be possible if the current 15% cant be sent...unless they are planning to perform some magic or witchcraft," said Ruto.

A week ago, the Council of Governors (CoG) decried delayed disbursement of funds from the the exchequer stating that most counties had not received their December and January monies.
