Burial of Kakamega mother of quintuplets canceled

- The burial was pushed to a later date to allow Kakamega county government complete construction of a house for the deceased's family

- The postponement will also allow the local administration finalise upgrading of a route heading to Namukhula's home

- A county Health Executive officer said the government will take care of the deceased's three surviving babies until they were 18 months old

The burial of Evelyn Namukhula, Kakamega woman who give birth to five babies, has been postponed to a later date.

Namukhula died in a Moi Teaching and Referral Hospital (MTRH) on Sunday, April 7 after developing pulmonary embolism, a blood clot blocked blood vessel in her lung.

Her burial which had been slated for Saturday, April 13, was postponed to allow the county government of Kakamega to complete construction of a house for the family, Daily Nation reported.

The government is also upgrading a road heading to the deceased's home in Sisokhe village.

Robert Makhanu, a county official, disclosed that the administration will take care of Namukhula's three surviving children until they were 18 months old.

According to Richael Okumu, the county Health Executive, the babies will be enrolled on Governor Wycliffe Oparanya's health initiative known as Oparanyacare Linda Afya ya Mama na Mtoto programme.

The county official further disclosed that the babies will be entitled to a monthly stipend of KSh5,000.

Namukhula gave birth to three girls and two boys at Kakamega County Referral Hospital.

The boys, however, died at MTRH where they had been transferred to after developing complications.
