Kagame tells off Richard Quest after questioning his long stay in power: &quot;Do I care?&quot;

- Quest was interviewing the president on his Quest Means Business TV show when he delved into matters of democracy

- Kagame reiterated that the Western world has its own good and bad things, which in no way makes it the yardstick for judging Africa

- Paul Kagame is the sixth president of Rwanda and has been at the helm of the landlocked country for 21 years

Rwanda President Paul Kagame has told-off celebrated CNN business reporter Richard Quest after the journalist questioned democracy levels in the East African country.

The business reporter was in Rwanda to interview the president for his Quest Means Business TV show when he delved into matters of democracy.

"The nature of elections is that people get tired of it and they move to another person," said Quest.

"That is if you're telling me that everyone has to conform to what the West tells us to do, I am not one of those people," interjected Kagame.

He added that the western world has its own good and bad things, which in no way makes it the yardstick for judging Africa.

"There is no western leader in a generally-accepted democracy that has been in office as long as you have," continued Quest."Do I care about that? A western leader is a western leader. I'm not one of those people who have to always conform to what the west tells us to do," maintained Kagame.

The 64-year-old maintained that democracy is not defined by the west because even whites oftentimes elect leaders then turn around and complain about the same choices.
