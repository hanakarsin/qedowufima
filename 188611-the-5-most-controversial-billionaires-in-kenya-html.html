5 controversial billionaires in Kenya, including Evans Kidero

- Julius Nyerere described Kenya as a country of 10 millionaires and 10 million paupers to signify the high level of inequality in the Kenyan society

- It was in the early 70s but even now, the figures could have only changed but Kenya remains a largely unequal society, thanks to corruption and plunder of public resources for selfish gain

1. Nairobi Governor Evans Kidero

He is considered as one of richest politician and investor in Nairobi with tangible investment portfolio in media,real estate,banking and agriculture.

But controversy seems to follow him wherever he goes.

READ ALSO: Esther Passaris exposes Kidero

While working as the chief executive of a now struggling miller, Mumias Sugar,Kidero was accused of swindling the company  billions of shillings through fraudulent transactions

A parliamentary draft report recommended for his prosecution.

During the 2013 General Elections,he was accused of bribing a supreme court judge Philip Tunoi with KSh 200 million to rule in his favour after Ferdinand Waititu challenged his win in court.Waititu is now the MP for Kabete

2. Harun Mwau

He is a prominent politician in Kenya with questionable business deals.

The US government has previously seized his assets and  issued a travel advisory against him in relations to his alleged involvement in drug trafficking.He is a former Kilome MP.

During the period of investigations,it emerged that he is a key investor at Nakumatt supermarkets, Mwundo Ltd, Africa Centre for Hope Initiative Everywhere.

Other companies he is linked with include Harun International Ltd, JH City Ltd, Preme Ltd, JN Records Ltd, Pepe Enterprises Ltd, Sheltown Ltd, Sports Champions Ltd, Tom Brown Ltd, Vitu Ltd and Kuston Kenya Ltd.

3. William Kabogo

The Kiambu governor is not new to controversy.

Just like Mwau,he has previously been linked to drug dealings.His investments' are said to be in real estate,agriculture and dairy farming.

4. Cyrus Jirongo

He made billions due to his close link with retired president Daniel Arap Moi.

Jirongo is reported to have investments in real estate and agriculture.

He has also gone public to declare that he made his first million before attaining the age of 30.

He was one of the six individuals mentioned  in the collapse of Dubai Bank.

Jirongo is also entangled in a row with Central Bank of Kenya  after the regulator threatened to sell his 1,000-acre land in Nairobi’s Ruai estate over a KSh 20 billion loan .

CBK was acting on behalf of the collapsed Postbank Credit.

5. Retired president Daniel arap Moi

Moi has huge investments in real estate,agriculture,media,manufacturing and  education.

He is the brain behind Kabarak Schools and is the majority shareholder at Standard Media Group.

The former president amassed his wealth during his 24-year-old reign,he is still facing some cases of fraud against him,underlying the fact that his wealth is questionable.
