Manchester City Star De Bruyne Rewarded with Lucrative New Deal

- De Bruyne is set to stay at the Etihad until his 34th birthday

- Manchester City announced it extended the Belgian’s deal after a string of solid performances

- The 29-year old was on target as City beat Borussia Dortmund 2-1 in the Champions League quarter final first leg

Manchester City midfielder Kevin de Bruyne has signed a new lucrative contract to stay at the Etihad until 2025.

City have decided to tie down the 29-year old further, despite him having two more years on his current deal.

The new deal means he will stay a Manchester City until his 34th birthday.

De Bruyne, who won the Premier League Player of the season award in the last campaign, expressed delight at signing a new deal, describing it as a “straight forward decision”.

"I could not be happier, since joining City in 2015, I have felt at home," De Bruyne said after his contract was confirmed. "I love the fans, my family are settled here in Manchester and my own game has developed really well,”"This football club is geared for success. It offers me everything I need to maximise my performance, so signing this contract was a straightforward decision. I am playing the best football of my career and I honestly feel there is more to come.

He joined Manchester City from German outfit Wolfsburg back in 2015 and since his second comeback to the Premier League, the Belgian has been a revelation at Manchester City.

So far, he has won the Premier League title twice, and has also scooped the FA Cup as well as the League cup four times.

He is on course to secure a third title and despite his 2020/2021 season being ravaged by injuries, he has managed eight goals and 16 assists in 33 appearances across all competitions this campaign.
