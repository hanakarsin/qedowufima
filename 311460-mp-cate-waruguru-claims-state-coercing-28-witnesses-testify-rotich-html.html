MP Cate Waruguru claims State coercing 28 witnesses to testify against Rotich

- Cate Waruguru claimed corruption charges against former Finance CS Rotich are political

- Rotich is accused of presiding over loss of KSh 19 billion meant for Arror and Kimwarer dams constructions

- He denied all the charges and was released on cash bail

Laikipia Woman Representative Cate Waruguru has claimed corruption cases facing the immediate Finance Cabinet secretary Henry Rotich has taken a political angle.

Speaking in Uasin Gishu on Friday, July 26, Waruguru dragged President Uhuru Kenyatta's name into the recent arrest and prosecution saying he was misled into believing lies and propaganda.

The former Laikipia nominated MCA further accused investigating agencies of playing politics and often targeting politicians viewed as anti-Jubilee government.

Waruguru challenged the Directorate of Criminal Investigations and the Directorate of Public Prosecution to produce concrete evidence linking Rotich to Arror and Kimwarer dam scandals.

It is alleged Rotich was significantly involved in illegal tendering and payment processes of the project which ignited political confrontations.

" Today, we are looking for enough evidence on Arror and Kimwarer dams without which we will not going to accept. We know there are 28 people who are being coerced to testify against Rotich," she claimed.

In particular, the outspoken politician who is also a member of team Tanga Tanga camp, called for fresh probe of Interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang'i over controversial Ruaraka land saga.

"We summoned Matiang'i to the parliamentary committee on land to answer to allegations of Ruaraka land. My friend, Matiang'i knows what happened. There is someone who is not telling Uhuru the truth," she added.

The government had agreed to purchase the over 13-acre piece of land at KSh 3.2 billion from developer Francis Mburu despite it legally being public property.

The then Education Cabinet Secretary Matiang'i paid Mburu KSh 1.5 billion in down payment for the parcel where two public schools stand.

Recently, a High Court ruled that payment of KSh 1.5 billion was irregular.
