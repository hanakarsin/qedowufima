Not an Issue: Magoha Downplays Closure of Uhuru Primary after 16 Pupils Tested Positive for COVID-19

  • Education CS George Magoha has dismissed the closure of a school in Nyandarua county after 16 pupils and four teachers tested positive for COVID-19
  • This was after about 70 of them are reported to have gone to Nyairoko Dispensary with diarrhea and sore throats
  • After a thorough investigation, 16 of them were found to be COVID-19 positive, prompting the school's indefinite closure
  • Magoha, however, said the government was doing everything necessary to secure the safety of all students across the country

The shutdown of a school in Nyandarua County due to COVID-19 has been dismissed by Education Cabinet Secretary George Magoha as a non-issue.

Mogoha made the remarks in Thika a day after Uhuru Primary School was briefly closed after 16 of the school's pupils and four staff were infected with the deadly disease.

About 70 learners from the public institution are said to have gone to Nyairoko Dispensary with diarrhea and sore throats.

Sixteen of them were found to be COVID-19 positive following a thorough investigation, prompting the school's indefinite closure.

“This is not an issue at all," he told journalists after witnessing ongoing interviews for the World Bank-funded Elimu Program that is giving 9,000 scholarships to vulnerable children.

"You remember the noise people made before. If the president was not bold enough to open schools, would these children be lining up here to get scholarships to go to high schools,” he added

However, the education secretary stated that the government is doing everything necessary to secure the safety of all students across the country.

He emphasized that the infection of around 10 students should have no bearing on the learning of 14 million pupils and that learning will continue.

Pupils placed under home based-isolation

TUKO.co.ke previously reported that the pupils, who were placed under home-based isolation, are monitored by health workers from the Nyandarua County Government.
