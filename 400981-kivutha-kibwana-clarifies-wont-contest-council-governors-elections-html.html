Kivutha Kibwana clarifies he won't contest in Council of Governors' elections

- Kakamega governor Wycliffe Oparanya has served as CoG chair for two terms and is not eligible for reelection

- Governors Mwangi wa Iria and Kivutha Kibwana have opted out of the race for CoG boss to concentrate on their 2022 presidential bids

- Kivutha said his team told him occupying a position in CoG would constitute a conflict of interest for a person who is eyeing presidency in 2022

Makueni Governor Kivutha Kibwana has said he will not be throwing his hat in the ring in the race to succeed his Kakamega counterpart Wycliffe Oparanya as chairman of Council of Governors (CoG).

TUKO.co.ke has learnt the election is slated for Friday, January 29, and apart from CoG chair, county bosses will also be voting for the whip, vice chairman and chairpersons and vice-chairs of 11 technical committees.

Oparanya has been at the helm of the council for two terms and is not eligible for reelection. Already, there is intense lobbying by governors who are eyeing the influential position.

Kibwana was among those rumoured to be interested in the seat but in a statement on Tuesday, January 26, said he was withdrawing from the race to concentrate on his 2022 presidential ambition.

The Makueni county chief, who has been serving the CoG as a whip, said his advisers told him to opt-out since serving in the council will constitute a conflict of interest for a person who is seeking to be elected president in 2022.

"I will not be a candidate for Council of Governors elections. As a 2022 presidential candidate, I have been advised by my team that serving in the next CoG executive can constitute a conflict of interest. I thank my colleagues for the opportunity to serve as chief whip," said Kibwana.

As of Tuesday, it had emerged Murang'a governor Mwangi wa Iria, who had been perceived as a frontrunner, had also opted out of the race to concentrate on the 2022 race to state house.

Mwangi has been serving as Oparanya's deputy.

With the current heated political environment surrounding the BBI referendum, a person who will clinch the position is likely to be a governor who is in sync with President Uhuru Kenyatta and ODM leader Raila Odinga.

The new CoG boss will be expected to continue hammering the BBI gospel, just like Oparanya, to ensure it culminates into a referendum and enacted in law.

Governors will also need a chair who will be able to take the Executive head-on matters to do with increasing county allocation as enshrined in the BBI.

Among governors interested in the seat are Anne Waiguru (Kirinyaga), James Ongwae (Kisii) and Uasin Gishu's Jackson Mandago.
