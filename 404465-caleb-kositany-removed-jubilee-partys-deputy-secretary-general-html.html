Caleb Kositany removed as Jubilee Party deputy secretary-general

- The Tanga Tanga allied lawmaker has since been replaced by his Cherengany counterpart Joshua Kutuny

- The outfits SG Raphael Tuju said the decision was made after a National Management Committee meeting

Soy MP Caleb Kositany has been ousted as the Jubilee Party deputy secretary-general.

The Tanga Tanga-allied lawmaker has since been replaced by his Cherengany counterpart Joshua Kutuny.

The outfits secretary-general Raphael Tuju in a statement dated Monday, March 1, said the decision was made after a National Management Committee meeting held on the same day at the party headquarters in Pangani.

"...in exercise of its mandate as provided for under Article 7(2) of the Jubilee Party Constitution, removed Caleb Kositany from the position of deputy secretary-general with immediate effect," read the statement in part.

Party disloyalty

Jubilee vice-chairman David Murathe had earlier revealed a plan by the ruling party to oust Kositany as the purge on Deputy President William Ruto's allies continues.

The former Gatanga MP, who has President Uhuru Kenyatta's ears, accused Kositany of decamping to and campaigning for United Democratic Alliance (UDA) candidates against the party position.

"You (Kositany) has said you are publicly campaigning for a UDA candidate. So why don't you leave Jubilee Party in peace? " He posed.

However, the Soy MP in his defence wondered why Murathe himself was not disciplined, yet he campaigned for an ODM candidate in the Kibra by-election.

"Do not bring me those kinds of threats...I am not that type...you were in Kibra campaigning for an ODM candidate so don't talk," Kositany argued.
