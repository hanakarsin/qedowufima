KNEC clarifies Grade 3 pupils sitting national assessment will not be ranked

The Kenya Early Years Assessment (KEYA) set to be undertaken by Grade Three pupils in September 2019 is a national assessment and not an examination.

Kenya National Examination Council (KNEC) on Monday, July 1, announced the first national assessment will also not be ranked.

Acting Chief Executive Officer (CEO) Mercy Karogo further noted the council will not release the results for either individual schools or learners, Daily Nation reported.

“The KEYA assessment tools shall be distributed to all the curriculum support officers, who shall coordinate with the head teachers in their respective zones to administer the assessment in September,” said Karogo.

She further noted the schools will administer and score the learning outcomes in the assessment tools.

She said a national assessment centre had already been established at KNEC and has been conducting various formative national assessments.

“As has been the case with national assessments, the outcomes of KEYA shall be compiled into a report, which shall provide feedback at the national level,” said Karogo.

The learners will be assessed in Early Years Education in Grade Three, Middle Years Education in Grade Six, Lower Secondary Education in Grade Nine and Senior School Education in Grade 12.

“KEYA, therefore, is an assessment to inform on learning achievements attained by learners in Grade Three,” Karogo explained.

She added the assessment is aimed at improving learning and instruction and facilitating identification and nurturing of every learner’s potential.

It will also facilitate selection of pathways and track in senior secondary schools, provide assessment feedback to learners and other stakeholders.
