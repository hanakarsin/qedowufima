Anyang' Nyong'o says cancer centres without specialists will not help fight disease

- The county boss said government needed to focus on research and specialisation of doctors

- He urged counties to collaborate with colleges and universities in carrying out research

- He noted the main challenge in fighting cancer was getting the right human resource

Kisumu governor Anyang' Nyong'o has shared his experience in the fight against cancer saying the government needs to concentrate more on research and training specialists rather than building centres.

While acknowledging the need for cancer centres, the county boss emphasised on the need for educating the public and training specialists just like in other developed nations.

Speaking on Saturday, August 3, during burial ceremony of the late Bomet governor Joyce Laboso who succumbed to cancer, Nyong'o said there is need to for colleges and universities to partner with counties to develop the needed human resource.

"What is important is getting people who are specialized in different types of cancer. In developed countries we have specialists, we need to focus research, education and specialisation,""The big job is training and research. Universities should collaborate with counties and work together to develop human resource. With cancer centres only we’ll not get it right- it is important but we need to stop myths so that we deal with real issues," said Nyong'o.

He also disclosed that just like a majority of other patients who have been cured of cancer, Laboso was only on remission with the disease having been realised in 1991.

Nyong'o is a prostate cancer survivor and he was diagnosed with the disease in July 2010 when it was at its second stage.

He was immediately put on hormone therapy to stop the spread while awaiting further treatment in the United States.

He went to US where he underwent intense modulated radiation therapy and was cured.
