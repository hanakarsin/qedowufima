Soludos Statment On Obis Investments Sign Of Jealousy, Malice Chukwumezie

The founder of Maka Odinma Ndigbo, Nze Kanayo Chukwumezie has said that Governor Charles Soludo’s statement about former Governor Peter Obi’s investments in Anambra is an indication that Soludo is jealous of the widespread acceptance which Obi enjoys ahead of the 2023 general elections.

It would be recalled that Soludo during an interview with Channels TV on Thursday had claimed that the investments being credited to Obi, when he held sway as Anambra governor, ‘are worth next to nothing’.

Soludo had stated that, “By the way, the investments that you’re talking about, I don’t know anything about them. I think there was something I read about somebody speculating about whatever investment. With what I’ve seen today, the value of those investments is worth next to nothing. So, let’s leave that aside.”

Reacting to the statement, Nze Kanayo Chukwumezie, while speaking with Journalists on Friday in Awka, stressed that Soludo may have an hidden agenda, hence the unruly and unprofessional statement.

He said: “The statement is very unruly, unprofessional and seems like he is either jealous of Obi’s exploits or that he has malice. It carries much undercurrent that will soon manifest itself.

“Soludo may be having a hidden agenda. It is a shocker and no one expects such from him, especially being the governor of Obi’s state, considering Obi’s acceptance across all ethnic groups, states, gender, age brackets and even political divides. Soludo has many explanations to make.”
