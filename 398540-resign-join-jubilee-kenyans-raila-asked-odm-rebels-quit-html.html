&quot;Resign and join Jubilee,&quot; Kenyans tell Raila after he asked ODM rebels to quit

A section of online users has turned the tables on Opposition leader Raila Odinga after he dared his outfit's rebels to ditch his party and seek re-election in parties of their choice.

Netizens instead challenged the Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) party leader to first ditch his party and join the ruling Jubilee outfit.

They claimed the former premier was the first person to betray his party after his handshake with President Uhuru Kenyatta.

They added Raila has gone ahead to support Dennis Waweru, a Jubilee candidate in the Nairobi gubernatorial race.

Reactions

Philis Ndiema said: "Baba handshake Jubilee and Aisha handshake Tangatanga what is the difference?"Mwangi Kibara opined: "You also joined your brother on the other side. Resign first and the rest will follow suit."Daniel Langat said: "And Raila has also switched ODM allegiance to Jubilee/Handshake."Milton Musalia opined: "Raila should resign and seek re-election on the Jubilee ticket because he himself joined Jubilee and he's championing a Jubilee candidate in Nairobi gubernatorial race. Likewise, Uhuru should resign and seek fresh re-election on the ODM ticket because he supported Boga to the extent of taking photos plus his portrait. If not, then handshake principals should give us a break. They can't preach water and take wine at the same time."

The Opposition chief said there were leaders in Kilifi county who claimed to be in ODM but they belonged to other parties.

"And across here (Kilifi) we have some rebel members who claim to be in ODM but they belong to other 'parties'. The right thing for them to do is resign and seek re-election in parties of their choice," said Raila.

The former premier was referring to Malindi Member of Parliament Aisha Jumwa.

The lawmaker is still a member of ODM despite attempts to expel her for supporting Deputy President William Ruto's 2022 presidential ambition.

The handshake

In the 2017 General Elections, Uhuru and Raila were on the opposite sides of the political divide and the country was polarised along tribal lines.

However, on the steps of Harambee House on March 18, 2018, the two leaders buried the hatchet in what came to be known as the handshake.

The handshake, which also birthed the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI), brought Raila and his lieutenants on board in running of government affairs while Deputy President William Ruto was pushed to the periphery due to his rejection of the truce.
