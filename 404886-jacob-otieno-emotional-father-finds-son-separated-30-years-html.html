An emotional dad has narrated events that led to finding his son after being separated for more than 30 years.

Jacob Otieno said the reconnection story started during the New Year period when the dad went to church and their parish had just received a new head.

Speaking to TUKO.co.ke, Otieno disclosed the new pastor noticed the church had no clock and asked congregants to volunteer and he was the only one who raised his hand.

A week later, he brought a new clock to the church and everyone applauded him for his kind gesture.

The following week, while going about his business as a tailor, a lady walked into the shop adjacent to his to get her phone fixed.

''I normally sew clothes outside in a verandah and this lady was walking into the phone repair shop and she stopped and stared at me for a long time and then went in.

A few minutes later, I walked in to iron a short I had just made and she greeted me and said I look like some she knows.However, I joked and said, may be we have met before and she is just confusing that moment with someone else,'' he told TUKO.co.ke.

But, the lady identified as Mary told Otieno that he looks like his former colleague who was an orphan and revealed his name as Victor Otieno.

''When she said his name, I was shocked and even got more serious because Victor is my son and I named him after my father Otieno. So I asked her if she got his phone number, she said she lost his contact but gave me his Facebook username,'' he explained.

Otieno married Victor's mother Tabitha as soon as she finished Class Eight. He said their union was hurriedly done because his lover was running away from her hostile step mother.

In 1988, the two welcomed a baby boy, Victor. But after a year, Tabitha ran away from her matrimonial home and returned to her dad's home.

Otieno upon seeing how serious things were took his dad and an elder from their village to go pay bride price so that he could get his wife back. But his wife never came back.

He tried begging, pleading and even sweet-talking her to return home with their son but his cry fell on dead ears.

Shortly, Tabitha's dad retired and so they had to relocate from their Kisumu Railways home to Funyula, after the dad bought a piece of land in that region.

He kept looking but later when he found their new residential home, he was told that Tabitha was married off to a different guy.

Otieno also decided to move on with his life, found a good woman and sired five children with her.

However, in mid-90s, while walking around Kisumu Boys High School round about, Otieno bumped into Loise, who is Tabitha's step sister who told her about his wife's whereabouts.

Later, he was shocked to find out that Tabitha had died shortly after returning to her father's home.

Her then husband and kids had died and so the family of the husband chased her away.

Right now, he is grateful that he has found his son after postponing plans to travel to Funyula to look for him.

''I was so happy because it was like a dream come true from God. Victor was told that his mom and dad were dead but here I am. I reached out to him on Facebook, went through his photos and even found one of my pictures his mum had left him.I travelled immediately to Nairobi to see and I am just glad I found him after a long period of time,'' he said.

In similar news, a Nairobi woman was thrilled after reuniting with family she had not seen for more than 15 years.

Esther Chebet said she was separated from her family in 2003 when she was still a child after her parents separated and her father, who worked in the army, took her together with her siblings from their mother and to their grandfather's home.

When he died, Chebet was taken by her aunt but she did not know who took her other siblings and she never saw them again until a radio presenter found them and reunited the whole family.
