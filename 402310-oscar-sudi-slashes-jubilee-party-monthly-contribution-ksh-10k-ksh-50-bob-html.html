Oscar Sudi slashes his Jubilee Party monthly contribution from KSh 10K to KSh 50 bob

- Oscar Sudi said from March 1, 2021, he will only be contributing KSh 50 to Jubilee Party, down from KSh 10,000

- Sudi said the balance will be channelled to Kazi Ni Kazi Welfare Group

- His Belgut counterpart Nelson Koech also announced he will also halt his monthly contribution to Jubilee

Kapseret MP Oscar Sudi has asked the Clerk of the National Assembly to slash his monthly contribution to the Jubilee Party.

The lawmaker said he wants the KSh 10,000 he contributes to the ruling party be slashed to KSh 50 and the balance channelled to Kazi Ni Kazi Welfare Group.

Sharing the letter he sent to the clerk, Sudi said he wants the changes made from March 1, 2021.

"I have officially written to the Clerk of the National Assembly to deduct my monthly contributions to Jubilee Party from 10,000/- to 50/-. I will channel the balance to Kazi Ni Kazi Welfare Group," he said.

Similarly, his Belgut counterpart Nelson Koech announced he will also halt his monthly contribution to Jubilee for the Kazi Ni Kazi Welfare Group.

"Effective today, I withdraw my Kshs 9,500 monthly contribution to the Jubilee Party and shall have the same channelled to the Kazi ni Kazi welfare group. We are walking the hustler talk in earnest," he said.

The lawmakers made the announcement the same day that they held a meeting with Deputy President William Ruto at his Karen residence.

Speaking after their meeting, Ruto allies warned their rivals that ejecting the second in command was not a walk in the park adding that the Amani National Congress (ANC) leaders who threatened to impeach the DP do not deserve a comment.

"Impeaching the DP is not a birthday party or a walk in the park. Go and dissect Ayb Savula and you will observe that the carrier of the matter does not deserve a comment," Aden Duale, who was flanked by more than 130 Tanga Tanga lawmakers said.
