Camila Cabello (Pop Singer) - Age, Family, Bio

First appeared as a member of the girl group Fifth Harmony on the second season of The X Factor in 2012, finishing in the top 3. Fifth Harmony's debut 2013 single "Miss Movin' On" was certified gold. She left the group in 2016 and released her debut studio album Camila in 2018, which debuted at #1 on the Billboard 200 chart. Her 2017 hit song "Havana" has been certified 7x Platinum. 

Before Fame

She first found a following by posting covers of popular songs to her YouTube channel. She'd never performed in front of anyone until her first X Factor audition. 

Trivia

As a solo artist while still in Fifth Harmony, she released singles such as "I Know What You Did Last Summer" and "Bad Things," which both entered the top 20 on the Billboard's Hot 100. She won two Latin Grammys in 2019 for Record of the Year and Best Pop Song for her collaboration with Alejandro Sanz on the single "Mi Persona Favorita." 

Family Life

She went back and forth between Havana, where she was born, and Mexico for the first 5 years of her life before moving to the U.S. Her full name is Karla Camila Cabello Estrabao. Her parents are Alejandro and Sinuhe Cabello and she has a younger sister named Sofia. She began a relationship with Shawn Mendes in 2019. They announced their breakup in November 2021. She previously dated Matthew Hussey for about a year. She also dated Lox Club CEO Austin Kevitch in 2022, but the couple has since separated. She got back together with Shawn Mendes in 2023 but they separated again after a few months. 

Associated With

In 2017, she collaborated with J Balvin and Pitbull on the song "Hey Ma," which was featured in the soundtrack for the film The Fate of the Furious.
